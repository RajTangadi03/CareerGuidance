Career Guidance

Explore different paths for your Destination.

A career guide not only helps a student decide the career route but also guides the student about the path, maps his/her career, helps him/her clear the confusion and distractions coming along the way.
The proper career guidance, ensures that the career chosen is the right one and thus results in emotional, social and financial happiness. Career guidance is the process to help individuals acquire information, skills and experience necessary to identify career options and narrow down to make a career decision.

Career in Science

  • Important career options in science include engineering, medicine, pharmacy, biotechnology, pure sciences (such as physics, chemistry, mathematics, and biology), agricultural sciences, environmental science, computer science, architecture, geology, oceanography, and forensic science.
  • There are many other career paths available in the field of science, depending on one's interests and aptitude.
  • You are probably already using scientific thinking in your everyday life maybe without even knowing it.

ENGG

Pursuing a Bachelor's degree in engineering is a common choice for students who have studied science in 12th grade.

Specializations include mechanical, civil, electrical, computer science, electronics, etc.

Architecture

For those interested in designing buildings and structures, pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Architecture (B.Arch) is an option.

Medicine

Students interested in healthcare can pursue MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery) or other related courses like BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery), BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery), BHMS (Bachelor of Homeopathic Medicine and Surgery), etc.

Career in Commerce

  • Involves managing money, investments, and financial assets, with career paths in banking, investment management, financial analysis, and corporate finance.
  • Focuses on recording, analyzing, and interpreting financial information, offering careers in auditing, taxation, financial reporting, and forensic accounting.
  • Centers on promoting products or services, with career options in advertising, brand management, market research, digital marketing, and sales management.

Finance

Professionals in finance may work in various roles, including financial analysis, investment management, corporate finance, banking, and financial planning. They analyze financial data, assess investment opportunities, manage financial portfolios, and provide strategic financial advice to organizations.

Accounting

Accountants prepare financial statements, maintain accounting records, analyze financial data, ensure compliance with tax laws and regulations, and provide financial advice to individuals and organizations.

Marketing

Marketing professionals conduct market research, develop marketing strategies, design promotional campaigns, manage product development, pricing, distribution, and branding, and build relationships with customers and stakeholders.

Philosophy
